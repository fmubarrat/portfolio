About Me

Hi, I'm Fayek. Currently, I serve as an Assistant Manager (Technical Lead) at Ernst & Young. However, I have discovered a new calling: applying AI to save lives, not just optimize business processes.

After five years building enterprise systems, I witnessed Dhaka's 2023 dengue outbreak claim over 1,600 lives. Clinics ran out of diagnostic supplies. Patients went untreated. I realized I needed to use my engineering skills for something more critical.

So, alongside my corporate responsibilities, I dedicated myself to computer vision. I built PlateletVision—a dengue diagnostic system achieving 96.56% detection accuracy. I validated the results mathematically, proving that rigorous engineering discipline can produce research-grade medical tools.

My goal is to bridge the gap between robust software architecture and advanced medical imaging to build resilient diagnostic systems for resource-limited settings.

Research Projects

My transition from Enterprise Engineering to Applied Research.

🩸 PlateletVision - Dengue Analyzer

AI-powered platelet morphometry system for dengue recovery prediction using microscopy images. Novel RBC-based auto-calibration eliminates manual microscope settings.

YOLOv8 OpenCV Medical Imaging Python Flask Angular
Detection Accuracy: 96.56% mAP@50
Morphometric Validation: 7.01 fL MPV
(clinical: 7.2-11.7 fL)
Processing Speed: 84ms per image (Real-time)
Dataset: BCCD (60 images, 907 cells)
Impact: Reagent-free testing ($0 vs $5-10 per test) enabling outbreak response in resource-limited settings.
